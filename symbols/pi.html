π (Pi)

Definition: Pi (π) is the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter. It is an irrational number, approximately 3.14159, and it never repeats or terminates.

Properties / Behavior:

  • π is irrational, meaning it cannot be expressed as a fraction of two integers.
  • π is transcendental, meaning it is not the root of any non-zero polynomial equation with rational coefficients.
  • It appears in geometry, trigonometry, calculus, probability, and even physics.
  • π has an infinite number of decimal places and no repeating pattern.

Examples of Usage:

Circumference of a circle: C = 2πr

Area of a circle: A = πr²

Volume of a cylinder: V = πr²h

Related Concepts:

  • Circle
  • Trigonometry (sin, cos, tan often use π)
  • Radians
  • Other mathematical constants: e, φ

Fun Fact: Pi Day is celebrated on March 14th (3/14) around the world!
